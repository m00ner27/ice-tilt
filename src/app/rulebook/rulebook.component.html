1. Introduction

1.1 Core Values and Purpose

The ITHL is a growing community full of people that want to have fun playing NHL with their friends while making new ones.

A lot of new members might not fully understand the intent of the ITHL and everyone needs to recognize what we are trying to do with the league before they get started with it.

The ITHL's main goal is to create a competitive environment for teams of all experience and skill levels. Super sweaty or brand new to the game, it doesn't matter - you will be considered a valued member of the league and community as long as you show respect to others.

Joining the ITHL allows players to get better at the game while meeting awesome people. We want the experience of playing in the ITHL to be like no other league. We put effort into making the league fun and engaging both on and off the ice.

The league is called the ICE TILT HOCKEY LEAGUE for a reason. We embrace and accept the fact that ice tilt is real. This game can create many unexpected and unique plays that end up tilting the ice in a certain direction. In some cases our rule book doesn't try and stop 'ice tilt' from happening, and that is important to understand.

The ITHL is meant for people to have fun with friends while playing EASHL. While we want people to take both the game and league seriously; at the same time we want people to understand that it isn't life or death. Things may happen throughout the season that are not accounted for in the rules. The Admin team will do their best to resolve every issue as fairly as possible.

Problems will come up throughout the season so it is important to be understanding, cooperative and reasonable when dealing with league issues.

1.2 ITHL Code of Conduct

  1. All team management, players, and league members are required to follow the ITHL Code of Conduct.
  2. Code of Conduct violations will be handled on a case by case basis, with disciplinary actions ranging from warnings, to suspensions, and in the worst of cases, bans.
  3. You are free to say whatever you want in the discord except for the following:
    • Promote other leagues or websites
    • Racist, sexist, homophobic, ableist, anti-semitic, promotion of use of drugs, or bigotry.
    • Political discussion/arguments.
    • Personal information of other members.
    • No personal attacks or threatening other players.
    • We are all here to have fun.
  4. Do not send members negative messages or harass them on Discord, Xbox Live, PSN, or any messaging app.
  5. Do not join other team's parties and harass them.
  6. Harassing other league members will not be tolerated. You can say your piece, but repeatedly messaging other league members may result in suspension, mute punishments and/or removal from league discord.
  7. Any member that wishes to make a complaint on another league member should notify an Admin AND create a ticket. Your messages will be responded to as soon as possible.

1.3 Bring Your Own Club

The ITHL is a 'Bring Your Own Club' League. It is important for everyone to understand that there are a wide variety of Clubs in the ITHL. Every team shares one thing in common; they are a group of friends that want to have fun playing competitive EASHL.

1.3.1 Club Make Up

The BYOC style only works if each team consists of people that get along and like each other. Please do not make a team full of skilled players that don't necessarily like or trust each other. Teams need to have a sense of cohesiveness, and players need to enjoy playing with each other.

The ITHL wants teams to have fun before sweating too hard. We appreciate teams that take the league seriously, but there must be some sort of 'Positive Team Culture' within your Club.

Feedback will be taken from GMs during the season to provide insight on how easy or hard it is to communicate with other GMs to set up games. Additionally, GMs will be able to provide feedback in the event of any "rage quitting", "trolling", or "griefing" during the season. If multiple issues arise regarding a particular team, the individuals involved from that team may not be allowed to participate in future seasons with their GM also not allowed to GM other teams.

1.4 League Issues

If you need help with anything in regards to the league, please create a #support-ticket on discord and an Admin will help you as soon as they can.

1.5 Admin Overrule

If any situation comes up throughout the season that is not covered in the rule book, the Admins will have the power to overrule it.

2. Expectations / Responsibility

2.1 Manager Responsibility

Before being accepted into the league as a General Manager (GM), an individual must fully understand the expectations and responsibilities that come with the position. To ensure this, prospective GMs must complete a rules-based screening process, which includes answering a series of questions about the league's rules. A minimum score of 80% is required to be approved as a GM.

Any player designated as an Assistant General Manager (AGM) on the website will be held to the same standards and responsibilities as a GM. This means that communication from an AGM with other teams will be considered official and treated with the same authority as communication from a GM.

While all players are expected to be familiar with the league rules, GMs have the added responsibility of ensuring their team members are well-informed throughout the season.

2.1.2 Communication

With the ITHL having 'no set game times', it is extremely important that GMs have the ability to communicate efficiently when setting up games with other GMs.

This includes:

  • Gathering availability before every league week (2.3 Availability).
  • Being easy to contact / responding to messages in a reasonable time frame.
  • Being reasonable when dealing with issues.

2.2 Club Commitment

Before applying to become a club, the GM of said club must fully know the commitment level of the club's players. We cannot have clubs be somewhat interested in the league. We need players and managers to understand that this is a respected league and if they don't show dedication towards it, they will be punished.

2.2.2 Folding Club Punishment

GMs who fold their club mid-season will be suspended from playing in the league for the remainder of that season plus the entirety of the following season.

If they want their suspension lifted they will have to pay a $40 fine.

Club members that aren't a manager will be allowed to sign with a different club.

2.2.3 Double Tagging

Definition: Double tagging refers to an individual using multiple accounts or gamertags to compete in the league simultaneously.

Any player caught double tagging will receive a permanent ban from the league. There will be no fines or temporary suspensions for this violation.

2.2.4 Gamertag Changes

If a player changes their gamertag, they must provide proof of the change. Proof may include a screenshot or documentation that verifies the new gamertag.

If a player cannot provide proof of the gamertag change, they will not be eligible to participate in league matches until proof is submitted and verified

2.3 Availability

League weeks will start on Mondays and end on the following Sunday (Monday-Sunday).

Clubs must be available to play at least 3 out of 5 nights every week (Monday - Thursday, Sunday).

Due to 'no set game times', being available 3 out of 5 nights every week ensures that games will be able to be played within the 7 day League Week.

That said, games can be played on Friday or Saturday but won't be counted towards Availability.

Availability will be after 9 EST every night. (The most reasonable time considering both East and West players).

If matchups cannot be scheduled within the given League Week, both team's availability will be reviewed by the Admin team. The team deemed to have more of a liability towards not being able to schedule a time will be forfeited. If it is both team's fault, 1 win and 1 loss will be given to each team.

If you do not have proof of your team's availability throughout the week, it may be used against you when deciding what team is at fault for not finding a game time. This is why we recommend teams format their availability like the picture attached above.

2.3.1 Playoff Availability

If a series cannot be completed within the 7 day Playoff League Week, forfeits will be given out.

2.4 Game Start Time

Clubs will be expected to be available to start games between 9:00 - 10:00 PM EST. If your club cannot be available to start playing games within this time period, you may be subject to forfeits if a game time cannot be decided on.

That said, games can be scheduled for any time.

This is the most reasonable time period to play games for both East and West players. We hope that forfeits in Playoffs don't occur, but this rule is just to make sure all bases are covered when looking at potential availability issues.

2.5 Setting Up Games

Each league week, every GM is responsible for arranging games with the two scheduled opponents.

Game Setup Requirements

  • Period length must be set to 4 minutes.
  • Grudge Matches must be turned off.

Scheduling Procedure

  • At the start of the week (Sunday night / Monday), each GM must message the opposing GM with all days and times their club is available.
  • Once a day and time have been agreed upon, one GM must post the confirmed game time in the #game-times channel on the ITHL Discord using the following format:
    Club 1 vs Club 2 – Wednesday 9 EST
  • Once posted, the time is considered official.
  • If for some reason a club wants to cancel the already confirmed time they must message the opposing club's GM more than 4 hours before said game time.
  • If the club doesn't cancel the game before the 4 hour mark they will have to forfeit both games.

Rescheduling & Availability

  • If a club needs to cancel a confirmed game, the GM must notify the opposing GM at least 4 hours prior to the scheduled start time.
  • GMs are expected to be available and respond to scheduling messages in a timely manner, with all clubs required to provide their availability no later than Monday night.
  • Failure to respond by this deadline will count as a day of unavailability for the club.
  • Continued lack of response or failure to provide reasonable availability may result in the club being declared unavailable, which can lead to forfeits for that week's scheduled games.

2.5.1 Setting Up Playoff Games

Every Playoff series will be a Best of 5. The entire series must be scheduled and played over a maximum of two nights.

This means that five games must be played entirely in one night or split into two.

EX) Night 1 - two games, Night 2 - three games.

Additionally, the amount of games played per night must be agreed upon. If the amount of agreed-upon games isn't met, the team that does not want to play will be subject to forfeit losses for the remaining games scheduled for that night.

2.5.2 Grace

If a club is 10 minutes late to the scheduled time, the club that is waiting will have the option to forfeit the opposing club for the first game scheduled.

If a club is 15 minutes late to the scheduled time, the club that is waiting will have the option to forfeit the opposing club for both games scheduled.

If a club shows up between 10-15 minutes and has forfeited Game 1, the club that wasn't late will be able to choose what server they want to play on for Game 2.

If a club takes longer than 10 minutes to be ready after Game 1 is played, the opposing club can choose to forfeit them for Game 2.

Evidence will be needed when forfeiting a team for violating grace.

EX) screenshot of messages, stream, etc.

2.5.3 Playoff Grace

In playoffs, the same grace period rules apply as outlined in section 2.5.2.

However, if a club fails to show up after the 15-minute grace period, they will forfeit all games that were agreed upon to be played in that series (up to a maximum of 3 games).

Evidence will be needed when forfeiting a club for violating playoff grace.

EX) screenshot of messages, stream, etc.

3. Housekeeping

3.1 Must Have 6 Players

This is a 6's League, meaning that every player on the ice must be human controlled when the game starts.

3.2 Withdrawing

If a team decides to withdraw after the season has started, as previously mentioned (2.2.2 Folding Club Punishment).

We work very hard to make this league run as smoothly as possible and when teams withdraw it creates a whole lot of work for us. It is also not fair to all of the other teams that have to adjust to teams leaving.

3.2.1 Previous Game Results Involving Teams That Have Withdrawn

In the event that a team officially folds during the season, the following measures will be taken to maintain fairness and competitive balance:

Game Result Adjustments

  • All past games involving the folded team will be converted to wins for the opposing (non-folded) team.
  • All future scheduled games against the folded team will be considered forfeit wins for the opposing team.

Player Statistics

All individual player statistics recorded in games involving the folded team will remain unchanged and will continue to count toward season totals.

Playoff Eligibility – GP Credit for Forfeits

For each forfeit win resulting from an unplayed game due to a team folding, the opposing team's General Manager may assign 2 Games Played (GP) to up to 6 players on their roster.

These GP credits are intended solely to support playoff eligibility requirements

4. General Rules

4.1 League Structure

  • • The IceTilt Hockey League consists of multiple divisions based on skill level
  • • Each division will have a maximum of 12 teams
  • • Regular season consists of 20 games per team
  • • Playoffs will include the top 8 teams from each division

4.2 Season Schedule

  • • Regular season runs for 10 weeks
  • • Games are scheduled for specific days and times
  • • Teams must be ready to play 15 minutes before scheduled start time
  • • No-shows will result in forfeit and potential disciplinary action

5. Team Management

5.1 Roster Requirements

  • • Each team must maintain a minimum of 12 players and maximum of 20 players
  • • At least 2 players must be designated as goaltenders
  • • Teams must have a minimum of 6 players present to start a game
  • • Roster changes must be submitted 24 hours before game time

5.2 Team Leadership

  • • Each team must have one designated General Manager
  • • GMs are responsible for roster management and team communication
  • • GMs must be active participants in league discussions
  • • GMs can be removed by league administration for rule violations

6. Player Rules

6.1 Player Eligibility

  • • Players must be 18 years or older to participate
  • • Players can only be on one team per season
  • • Players must complete registration and provide valid identification
  • • Players with outstanding league fees are ineligible to play

6.2 Player Conduct

  • • Players must respect opponents, officials, and spectators
  • • No fighting or physical altercations will be tolerated
  • • Players must follow all safety protocols and equipment requirements
  • • Unsportsmanlike conduct will result in immediate ejection

7. Game Rules

7.1 Game Format

  • • Games consist of three 20-minute periods with 2-minute intermissions
  • • Running clock except for the final 2 minutes of the third period
  • • Overtime: 5-minute sudden death period if game is tied
  • • Shootout: Best of 3 rounds if overtime doesn't decide winner

7.2 Penalties

  • • Minor penalties: 2 minutes
  • • Major penalties: 5 minutes
  • • Misconduct: 10 minutes
  • • Game misconduct: Ejection from game

8. Trades & Transactions

8.1 Trade Rules

  • • Trades must be approved by both team GMs
  • • Trades must be submitted through the official league platform
  • • Trade deadline is 48 hours before playoffs begin
  • • No trades allowed during playoffs

8.2 Free Agent Signings

  • • Free agents can be signed at any time during regular season
  • • Teams must have roster space available
  • • Free agent signings must be reported within 24 hours
  • • Players released from teams become free agents immediately

9. Code of Conduct

9.1 Sportsmanship

  • • All participants must maintain high standards of sportsmanship
  • • Respect for opponents, officials, and league staff is mandatory
  • • Discrimination of any kind will not be tolerated
  • • Positive attitude and fair play are expected at all times

9.2 Communication

  • • All league communication must be respectful and professional
  • • No harassment, threats, or inappropriate language
  • • Disputes should be resolved through proper channels
  • • Social media conduct must reflect league values

10. Penalties & Discipline

10.1 Disciplinary Actions

  • • First offense: Warning and documentation
  • • Second offense: 1-game suspension
  • • Third offense: 3-game suspension
  • • Fourth offense: Season suspension or expulsion

10.2 Appeal Process

  • • All disciplinary actions can be appealed
  • • Appeals must be submitted within 48 hours
  • • Appeals will be reviewed by league administration
  • • Final decisions are binding and non-negotiable

11. Appeals Process

11.1 How to Appeal

  • • Submit written appeal to league administration
  • • Include all relevant evidence and documentation
  • • Appeals must be submitted within 48 hours of decision
  • • Appeals will be reviewed within 72 hours
